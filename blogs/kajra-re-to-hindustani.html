The Tinted Lens

From "Kajra Re" to Hindustani Classical

June 11, 2026 • Reflection

Usually everyone is exposed to some or the other kind of music at a very young age, for me that being Bollywood and Indian Classical Music. When I was around 3 years old, I was hooked on both.

Music as Memory

Music often wraps memories around it and stores it in the brain. Sometimes I listen to music to store memories and emotions and relive those moments later. Whenever I play those songs, I'm instantly transported back to that moment in time.

The Language of Instruments

Different instruments wield distinct influences over me. Sitar recitals help me concentrate on my work while flute and piano compositions usher in a sense of serenity. I often find myself reaching for specific instruments depending on my emotional state.

Music as Catharsis and Navigation

Whenever I feel overwhelmed, I sing my heart out. In the realm of Hindustani Classical Music, each Raag embodies a spectrum of emotions. This diversity allows me to navigate through my feelings with precision.

"Music is the thread that connects my past to my present, turning every emotion into a melody I can understand."

Beyond Entertainment

To conclude, music's role in my life extends far beyond mere entertainment; it stands on par with my academic pursuits, possessing the potential to shape my day and infuse depth and serenity into my existence.
