The Tinted Lens

From "Kajra Re" to Hindustani Classical

June 11, 2026 • Reflection

Usually everyone is exposed to some or the other kind of music at a very young age, for me that being Bollywood and Indian Classical Music. When I was around 3 years old, I was hooked to the song "Humma" from Bombay and "Kajra Re". When I was younger, around the age of 4 or 5, the goal was just to groove to the music and dance along, but I recall my music tastes shifting when I started taking Hindustani Classical Music classes. I began to gravitate towards mellow, melodious music. My parents then exposed me to instrumental music, and "Yeh Jo Des Hai Tera" from Swades quickly became a favourite. When I studied Hindustani Classical Music in depth, I eventually came to the realisation that music has far more value than just enabling dancing or fostering a groovy feeling.

Music often wraps memories around it and stores it in the brain. Sometimes I listen to music to store memories and emotions and relive those moments later. Whenever I play those songs, it takes me back in time. However, I don't listen to music just for a purpose as shallow as this. It brings me quiet, new insights, and a sense of calmness. Different instruments wield distinct influences over me. Sitar recitals help me concentrate on my work while flute and piano compositions usher in a sense of serenity. I often find it challenging to articulate my feelings of sadness. The soulful notes of a shehnai composition provide me with a gentle means to understand and express these emotions, allowing me to embrace my vulnerability in a more compassionate and human way. There are some songs that I specifically listen to for the lyrics.

Whenever I feel overwhelmed, I sing my heart out. In the realm of Hindustani Classical Music, each Raag embodies a spectrum of emotions. This diversity allows me to navigate through various moods and states of mind. To conclude, music's role in my life extends far beyond mere entertainment; it stands on par with my academic pursuits, possessing the potential to shape my day and infuse depth and serenity into my thoughts.

"Music is the thread that connects my past to my present, turning every emotion into a melody I can understand."
