The Tinted Lens

From "Kajra Re" to Hindustani Classical

June 11, 2026 • Reflection

Usually everyone is exposed to some or the other kind of music at a very young age, for me that being Bollywood and Indian Classical Music. When I was around 3 years old, I was hooked to Bollywood music. My mother would hum songs, my father would play the sitar, and I would be lost in this world of melodies. Listening to "Kajra Re" from the movie Bunty Aur Babli became my first musical memory. The song's charm captivated my young mind, introducing me to the world of Hindustani music seamlessly wrapped in contemporary beats.

Music often wraps memories around it and stores it in the brain. Sometimes I listen to music to store memories and emotions and relive those moments later. Whenever I play those songs now, I'm transported back to my childhood - to the warmth of my parents' presence and the simplicity of those early years. Each note carries a fragment of that time, and in listening to them again, I'm not just hearing music; I'm revisiting moments I hold dear.

"In every raag, there's a story waiting to be told—a conversation between the soul and the strings."

Whenever I feel overwhelmed, I sing my heart out. In the realm of Hindustani Classical Music, each Raag embodies a spectrum of emotions. This diversity allows me to navigate through my feelings with precision and grace. Raag Bhairav at dawn brings tranquility, while Raag Yaman in the evening kindles hope. The classical tradition offers a sophisticated emotional vocabulary that goes beyond words—a language that speaks directly to the soul.

Music's role in my life extends far beyond mere entertainment; it stands on par with my academic pursuits, possessing the potential to shape my day and infuse depth and serenity into even the most challenging moments. Whether through the nostalgic strains of Bollywood or the intricate beauty of Hindustani classical music, every melody is a thread connecting me to my heritage, my emotions, and ultimately, to myself.

Every note, a reflection of the soul.
