The Tinted Lens

Paralysis Analysis

June 11, 2026 • Reflection

It has been so long since I wrote anything. Lately, I feel like I'm living on top of a giant pile of shelved dreams and forgotten hobbies.

I look around my room and see the casualties of my own curiosity. So many books, bought and unread. So many fascinating things left unlearnt. Dusty music instruments, loved but rarely played.

Even my own body feels like a collection of working organs running on auto-pilot.

The blog page I wanted to host, the card game I wanted to design, learning fingerstyle on the guitar, painting, that 1000-piece puzzle... and just like that the 'someday' list keeps on getting out my of hands.

It's been a year since I cleared CFA Level 1, and honestly, I feel like I have nothing to show for it. Naught.

I was never like this—at least, not for the longest time I can remember. All my life I have been after something or the other. Somewhere along the way, I got buried under a mountain of my own options, paralyzed by everything I could be doing until I ended up doing nothing at all.

Despite having a profuse amount of time on my hands, I am severely lacking the motivation to actually get things done.

Every day feels exhausting, and my habits are all over the place. I don't entirely remember how I managed to get here.

"Too much freedom can feel like a cage. When every path is open, standing still feels safer than picking the wrong one."

All my time goes into deciding what activities will maximize my productivity. Little do I know!

What do I do? How do I fix this? Where do I even start? How do I think my way out of this thinking trap?

Maybe pick a book and read just 10 pages? Or play something I already know on the guitar for 5 minutes? Re-do that 250-piece puzzle? Paint the smaller canvas?

Or write a blog on Word, look at it, and smile instead of waiting for my website to come to life? 😊
